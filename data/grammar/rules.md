# Grammar Rules

| Topic | Explanation | Examples | Level |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------|
| Nominative Case | The nominative case marks the subject of a sentence — the person or thing performing the action. | Der Mann schläft. Die Frau lächelt. | A1 |
| Akkusativ (Accusative) | The accusative marks the direct object — the receiver of the action. Masculine articles change: der → den. | Ich sehe den Mann. Er kauft einen Kaffee. | A1 |
| Dativ (Dative) | The dative marks the indirect object — to whom or for whom an action is done. | Ich gebe dem Mann das Buch. Er hilft der Frau. | A2 |
| Genitiv (Genitive) | The genitive shows possession or relationship. Often replaced by von + dative in spoken German. | Das Auto des Mannes. Die Tasche der Frau. | B1 |
| Konjunktiv II | Used for hypothetical statements, polite requests, and indirect speech. Formed from past tense stem + umlaut. | Ich würde gern kommen. Könnten Sie mir helfen? | B1 |
